Altered self-perception ?

Altering self-perception is a common issue on social media, where users can present a polished or embellished version of their lives online. On Facebook, this can have negative consequences for users' mental health and well-being. Here are some key figures that illustrate the extent of this phenomenon.

Firstly, according to a survey conducted by the Royal Society for Public Health in the UK, social media, including Facebook, was rated as the worst influence on young people's body image. Facebook users may be influenced by photos and videos of their friends presenting perfect bodies and lives, which can lead to feelings of insecurity and inadequacy.

Moreover, according to a study by Stanford University, Facebook users tend to present themselves more positively online than in person. The study found that users tended to present a more embellished and positive image of themselves on Facebook, which can lead to a distortion of self-perception and pressure to maintain this online image.

Furthermore, a survey by Pew Research Center revealed that 43% of Facebook users had seen friends post content that did not correspond to their real life. Additionally, 39% of users reported seeing friends post content that made them feel uncomfortable or inadequate.

However, it is worth noting that Facebook has implemented measures to combat altered self-perception, such as the ability to hide posts from specific friends or reduce the amount of content that appears on the news feed. Additionally, some studies have shown that Facebook users who have a positive self-image online tend to have a better self-image in real life.

In conclusion, altering self-perception is a common issue on Facebook, which can have negative consequences for users' mental health and well-being. Although Facebook has implemented measures to combat this phenomenon, it is important for users to be aware of the potential negative effects of altered self-perception on Facebook and to take steps to protect their mental health and well-being.
